The Environmental Policy Comparator

Let's democratise scientific knowledge on Environmental Policies!

Why an Environmental Policy Comparator?

The Idea

The project aims at developing of a web-based tool allowing users to access information about the effectiveness of environmental policies based on systematic reviews and meta-analyses of the scientific literature. This tool aims at democratising access to scientific evidence and sources with the end goal of improving policy decisions pace and quality.

The Content

To deliver a first prototype by the end of the academic year 2020-2021, the project will first be narrowed to a selected set of environmental policies. The first objective will be to develop a user-friendly interface around which will be developed the back-end components of the project in a second stage. Starting from a prototype interface, the long-term objective is two-fold:

  • Publish a proof of concept by the end of the academic year 2020-2021
  • Begin a working paper about the back-end tools allowing for the website to work. This working paper will be the methodological appendix to the project.
The back-end tools are the different applications and features embedded in the prototype that transform raw data into a user-friendly interface (the latter being called the front-end). Many tools could be developed around this project, from the automatised selection of relevant articles to their summarising, but we will focus on the development of a relevant impact index and policy characterisation criterion for this proof of concept. Further automation could be developed; we leave that for latter potential research and development.

Development Schedule - Proof of Concept

First stage: Creating a prototype project - In progress

The first stage of the project will be devoted to the development of the tool used to share information with users. Some desired key features have been discussed during the first meeting:

  • Configurability by the user: selection of the relative importance of the different criterion
  • Policy assessment based on: cost, impact evaluated, implementability, degree of scientific confidence, “politique vecteur” (translation of this concept?)
  • Importance of visualisation: creation of graphical representation of indexes, importance of the interpretability and comparability
However, some work is still needed:
  • To get inspired by “good-practices” from similar tools existing for other topics (benchmark)
  • Define the exact index over which policies will be compared (communication and statistics)
  • Define the parameters users can change (communication and statistics)
  • Develop the first interface of the prototype (web-development work)
  • Refine the tool name
  • Deterline the first policies presented in the tool

Second Stage: Developing the Data Selection Protocol - Not started

  • Define a Policy Characterisation Framework
  • Construct an index for impact evaluation: from articles results to meta-analysis
  • Define a first-best systematic review methodology (including comprehensive literature review) and propose a second-best strategy for the proof-of-concept, with proposals to improve accuracy with further development after presentation of the prototype if the project is further developed.

Third Stage: Testing the Tool from the Administrator Perspective: growing the database - Not started

  • Define the subset of policies included in the prototype
  • Apply the second-best protocol defined in the second stage to grow the database
  • Implement the most important and most feasible improvements needed.
  • Summarize challenges faced in this third stage as an “Improvements needed” chapter in the working paper

Fourth Stage: Presenting the Proof of Concept Project - Not started

  • Think about the future of the Project: was it a good idea? If yes: Further development priorities? Partnerships? Funding (for hosting needs)?
  • Publish the tool online: hosting costs
  • Publish the working paper as a methodological appendix to the Prototype
  • Presentations / Call for Data?
